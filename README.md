# Aegis Research

> **Evidence over assumptions. Reasoning over memorization. Progress over perfection.**

Aegis Research is my personal malware research lab and investigation archive dedicated to understanding how malware behaves through evidence-driven investigation. Malware samples are analyzed to understand how they operate, why they were designed to behave as they do, and what evidence supports those conclusions.

Every investigation published by Aegis Research serves two purposes: to document my continuous development as a malware researcher and to provide a technical reference that may assist other security professionals in their own investigations. The objective is not to establish absolute truth, but to present detailed, reproducible research that others can validate, challenge, and build upon.

---

# Research Philosophy: 
A common set of research standards guides every investigation published by Aegis Research. These principles promote disciplined analysis, intellectual honesty, and a repeatable methodology regardless of the malware family, tooling, or investigative scope.

## Evidence over assumptions

Every statement presented in an investigation should be traceable to supporting evidence. Observations, analysis, and conclusions are supported by artifacts collected throughout the investigation rather than assumptions or unsupported claims.

## Reasoning over memorization

Knowledge provides context, but it should never replace critical thinking. Existing knowledge should be used to reason through unfamiliar problems by questioning assumptions, testing hypotheses, and allowing the available evidence to guide the investigation.

## Progress over perfection

Research is iterative. Every investigation represents the current understanding of a malware sample based on the available evidence at the time of analysis. The objective is continuous improvement—not perfection—through reflection, experience, and refinement of investigative methodology.

# Research Methodology

Every investigation follows a structured methodology designed to reduce assumptions, improve reproducibility, and ensure conclusions are supported by observable evidence. While the technical workflow may vary depending on the malware sample, the underlying research process remains consistent.

1. Observe
2. Form hypotheses
3. Identify assumptions. Gather evidence. Test hypotheses 
6. Revise conclusions 
7. Document findings 
8. Identify research and defensive opportunities. Conduct a peer review.

This methodology provides a consistent framework for approaching unfamiliar malware while remaining flexible enough to adapt to different malware families, operating systems, and research objectives.

# What You'll Find Here

Every investigation is intended to provide a structured, evidence-driven analysis of a malware sample. Although each investigation is unique, reports may include:

* Research objectives and investigative scope
* Static analysis
* Dynamic analysis
* Behavioral observations supported by evidence
* Reverse engineering findings, when applicable
* Indicators of Compromise (IOCs)
* MITRE ATT&CK mappings, where appropriate
* Research findings and supporting evidence
* Remaining unknowns and investigative limitations
* References and supporting resources

Not every investigation will contain every section. The contents of each report are determined by the malware sample, the available evidence, and the scope of the investigation rather than a rigid template.
