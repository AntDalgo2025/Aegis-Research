# Aegis-Research
> **Evidence over assumptions. Reasoning over memorization. Progress over perfection.**

Aegis Research is an independent malware research lab and investigation archive dedicated to understanding how malware behaves and the techniques used to achieve its objectives. Every investigation is conducted using an evidence-driven methodology that emphasizes observation, hypothesis testing, and technical analysis over assumptions.

The purpose of Aegis Research is to produce clear, reproducible investigations that explain what a malware sample does, how it operates, and what evidence supports those conclusions. Each investigation is documented to help other security professionals understand malware behavior and make informed defensive decisions based on the findings.

Research Philosophy

Every investigation published by Aegis Research is guided by a common set of research standards. These principles exist to promote disciplined analysis, encourage intellectual honesty, and establish a repeatable methodology for future investigations.

Evidence over assumptions

Every statement presented in an investigation should be traceable to supporting evidence. Observations, analysis, and conclusions should be supported by artifacts collected throughout the investigation rather than assumptions or unsupported claims.

Reasoning over memorization

Knowledge provides context, but it should never replace critical thinking. Every investigation should approach familiar and unfamiliar problems with the same analytical mindset—questioning assumptions, testing hypotheses, and allowing the available evidence to guide the investigation.

Progress over perfection

Research is an iterative process, and every investigation represents a snapshot of the current understanding of a malware sample. The objective is not to produce a perfect analysis, but to continuously refine investigative methodology, technical knowledge, and analytical reasoning through experience and reflection.
